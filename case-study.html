The Core Premise

The Bottleneck

Direct trains between major cities hit 100% capacity weeks in advance, leaving travelers forced to buy expensive last-minute flights.

✓

The Smart Route

By breaking the journey into 2 or 3 smaller legs via intermediate hubs, we can find confirmed seats that algorithms ignore.

User Research & Validation

Direct Train Availability (Delhi to Patna)

The "Tatkal" Desperation

User interviews revealed that over 70% of students and migrant workers panic-buy flight tickets at 3x the normal price when their emergency Tatkal railway tickets fail to confirm.

"I just need to get home for Diwali. I don't care if I have to change trains twice in random cities, as long as I have a confirmed seat." — User Interview #12

Product & MVP Scope

Building a robust routing engine without over-engineering the initial release.

Core User Experience

  • Above-the-Fold Search Users see the search widget immediately on load with popular route shortcuts to reduce friction.
  • Vertical Timelines Routes are displayed with vertical steppers clearly showing Layover times and Safety Buffers.
  • Geographic Filtering Algorithm physically restricts layover hubs to lie within a strict directional ellipse between Source and Destination.

MVP Prioritization (MoSCoW)

Must Have

Geographic corridor logic, Realistic IRCTC slab-based pricing.

Should Have

"Flight Cost Comparison" badge, Vertical Timeline UX.

Could Have

User accounts, saved favorite routes.

Won't Have (V1)

Direct Payment/Booking Integration (Too much API/legal overhead for MVP).

GTM Strategy & KPIs

Focusing heavily on festival seasons where the pain point is most acute.

North Star Metric

Routes Unlocked

Number of users who found a viable multi-leg route when the direct route was 100% waitlisted.

Secondary KPIs

  • Financial Impact Total ₹ saved vs alternative flights.
  • Time-to-Value Seconds from page load to first search (Optimized via above-fold form).

Hidden Inventory Discovery (Delhi → Chennai)

Showing how splitting legs drastically increases probability of finding seats.

Phased Go-To-Market Rollout

Phase 1: The Festival Test

Launch strictly 1 month before Diwali, targeting students via college subreddits complaining about Waitlists.

Phase 2: Specific Corridors

Focus SEO and localized ads purely on the "Delhi to UP/Bihar" and "Bangalore to Chennai" high-traffic corridors.

Phase 3: Meta-Search Partnerships

Provide our routing algorithm as an API to existing ticket platforms (MakeMyTrip) to monetize B2B.

Roadmap & Experimentation

Planned A/B Tests

Experiment: The "Flight Savings" Badge

Hypothesis: Dynamically fetching flight prices and displaying a badge saying "Save ₹3,200 vs Flight" on the train route will increase user intent-to-book by 15%.

Experiment: Layover Buffer Transparency

Hypothesis: Explicitly labeling a 3-hour wait as a "Guaranteed Safe Buffer for Train Delays" instead of just "Layover" will reduce user anxiety and increase adoption of 2-leg routes.

Risk Mitigation Matrix

Risk: Train 1 is delayed, missing Train 2

Mitigation: Hard-code a minimum 3-hour buffer into the routing algorithm. Do not show routes with tight connections.

Risk: Users booking wrong stations

Mitigation: Provide direct 1-click external links pre-filled with the exact station codes for both legs.
